# Characters — Overview

## Design Philosophy

Five playable characters, each built around a different axis of the combat system. Swapping characters in the party changes *how you play*, not just what numbers appear. Every character has a clear vulnerability that other characters cover, creating meaningful party composition decisions.

Stats are a character's **identity**, not something that normalizes over time. Stat growth is minimal (see [Progression](../progression/overview.md)) — a character's spread at level 1 defines who they are at level cap.

## Party Composition Dynamics

No single character is self-sufficient. The party composition creates a **defensive lattice**:

- **Physical damage** is handled by Ren (big HP pool) or Sera (Veil redirects to MP).
- **Elemental damage** is handled the same way — Sera's Veil absorbs it, Ren tanks through HP.
- **Psychic damage** is the wildcard — it always hits MP. Ren's tiny MP pool makes him vulnerable; Sera's Veil doesn't help. Kael and Maren have moderate pools. Lira has good MP but uses it for offense.
- **Status conditions** (Bleed, Burnout, Cursed) are handled by Maren's cleanse abilities. Without Maren, status-heavy encounters become attrition traps.
- **Attrition** in long fights favors characters with high recovery stats (Ren's Stamina, Sera's Spirit). Kael needs to end fights before his modest pools run dry.

The player must read the enemy encounter and choose the right 3-4 characters (party size TBD — see [Open Questions](../open-questions.md)) for the threats present.

# Chain Strike Acquisition

Chain Strikes are **found in the world**, not unlocked by level. Each character has a fixed number of Chain Strike slots (2–5 depending on their physical/magical focus), and the Chains that fill those slots are discovered through exploration.

## Discovery Methods

### Exploration rewards
A Chain Strike scroll found in a hidden cave, behind a puzzle, or in a chest off the critical path. Rewards curious players who poke at the world.

### Quest rewards
An NPC swordmaster teaches a Chain after the player completes a quest. These quests can themselves involve combat challenges — e.g., "defeat this enemy using only counters" to learn [Kael's](../characters/kael.md) *Riposte Cadence*.

### Boss drops
Signature Chains dropped by memorable bosses. The Chain thematically mirrors the boss's fighting style — you learn from your enemy. Examples:
- [Ren's](../characters/ren.md) *Threadcutter* drops from a speed-type boss, teaching him a fast Bleed-inflicting Chain.
- [Lira's](../characters/lira.md) *Cognitive Fracture* drops from a psychic boss, teaching her a Spirit-draining Chain.

### Environmental teaching
A Chain Strike pattern is carved into a dungeon wall or demonstrated by a training construct. The player must practice it on a training dummy in the room to learn it. This doubles as a tutorial for the Chain's timing — the player has already practiced the rhythm before they ever use it in combat.

## Distribution Pacing

- Characters start with **1 Chain** (their starter — see individual character files).
- A **second Chain** is available in the first major area.
- Roughly **1 Chain per major region/act** thereafter.
- By endgame, each character has their full roster: 2 ([Sera](../characters/sera.md)) to 5 ([Ren](../characters/ren.md)), reflecting their physical/magical focus.

## Character Specificity

Each Chain Strike is designed for a **specific character** (weapon and fighting style compatibility). A scroll found in a dungeon might be usable only by Ren (sword-type) or only by Kael (dueling-type). This prevents a single exploration sweep from filling every character's roster at once and gives each character their own reasons to explore.

## Interaction with Other Systems

- Found Chains start at [Mastery Level 1](mastery-system.md) — the player must use them to unlock branch inputs and Perfect+ tiers.
- Some Chains have **status effects on completion** (Bleed, Burnout, Drained) that synergize with [Lira's](../characters/lira.md) psychic drain strategy or [Maren's](../characters/maren.md) cleanse utility.
- Chain Strike HP costs interact with the [Two-Gauge System](../battle/stats-and-resources.md#the-two-gauge-system) — a character must consider whether they can afford a Chain without eating into Reserve.

# Sheet Music Acquisition

Spells are learned by finding **Sheet Music** — physical items in the world that teach a character a new spell. This replaces spell shops and level-up spell lists entirely. Every spell in the game exists as a findable item somewhere in the world.

## Discovery Methods

### Dungeon treasures
Sheet Music hidden in optional areas, behind puzzles, or in locked rooms. The most common source — rewards thorough exploration of each dungeon.

### NPC interactions
A bard in a tavern teaches a spell. A hermit mage on a mountain peak shares a rare score. A ghost in a ruin hums a melody that becomes Sheet Music. These encounters reward talking to NPCs and revisiting areas after story events.

### Environmental puzzles
A magical organ in a cathedral plays a melody — the player must reproduce it using the rhythm interface (a preview of the spell's actual pattern) to claim the Sheet Music. This teaches the player the spell's rhythm *before* they use it in combat, turning acquisition into a practice session.

### Quest chains
A multi-step quest to reconstruct a damaged Sheet Music by finding its fragments across multiple locations. The most powerful spells (Tier 3) require this kind of investment — they are not single pickups but narrative journeys.

### Boss drops
Rare, powerful spells from defeated magical enemies. Thematically appropriate — defeat a fire elemental, learn its signature fire spell.

## Elemental Identity Through Discovery

Each element's Sheet Music tends to be found in thematically appropriate locations:
- **Fire** spells in volcanic areas, forges, or places of conflict
- **Frost** spells in tundra, mountain peaks, or ancient tombs
- **Lightning** spells in storm-wracked regions or near ancient technology
- **Earth** spells underground, in caves, or in old-growth forests
- **Psychic** spells in places associated with madness, isolation, or ancient minds

This makes the world's geography a menu of magical options — the player learns to associate regions with the kind of power they'll find there.

## Character Compatibility

Not every character can learn every Sheet Music. A character's magical archetype determines which schools and tiers they can access:

| Character | Elemental Access | Psychic Access | Max Tier |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Ren](../characters/ren.md) | 1–2 basic spells | None | Tier 1 |
| [Sera](../characters/sera.md) | Full (8–10 spells) | None | Tier 3 |
| [Kael](../characters/kael.md) | 2–3 utility spells | 1–2 basic | Tier 2 |
| [Lira](../characters/lira.md) | 1–2 basic spells | Full (4–6 spells) | Tier 3 |
| [Maren](../characters/maren.md) | 2–3 damage + support | 1 defensive | Tier 2 |

When a character finds Sheet Music they can't use, it remains in the party inventory for another character to learn from. Nothing is wasted.

## Interaction with Other Systems

- Found spells start at [Mastery Level 1](mastery-system.md) — the player must cast them successfully to unlock the harder rhythm pattern and S-rank bonus.
- Spell MP costs interact with the [Two-Gauge System](../battle/stats-and-resources.md#the-two-gauge-system) — expensive spells on characters with small MP pools are risky (overflow into MP Reserve).
- [Groove Meter](../battle/rhythm-magic.md#groove-meter-working-name) rewards frequent casting, so having more spells in the repertoire gives more opportunities to build Groove.

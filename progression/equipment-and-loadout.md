# Equipment & Loadout

Equipment provides **tactical modifiers** — things that change how a character interacts with the game's systems. Equipment does not provide flat stat bonuses or raw power increases.

## Design Principle

A piece of equipment should change *how you play*, not *how hard you hit*. The best equipment creates new decision points or opens up strategies that weren't previously available. The worst equipment (which should not exist in this game) is "+10 Attack."

## Equipment Categories

### Weapons

Modify Chain Strike properties. Do NOT add flat damage.

Example effects:
- "Final hit of any Chain Strike has a 30% chance to inflict Bleed"
- "Chain Strikes of 4+ hits refund 5 Active HP on completion"
- "Reduce the HP cost of all Chain Strikes by 5" (efficiency, not power)
- "Counter-attacks deal bonus damage equal to 10% of damage prevented" (rewards Kael's counter play)

### Armor

Modify defensive properties and damage routing.

Example effects:
- "Widen Guard window by 10%" (accessibility, not invincibility)
- "Stagger no longer stuns" (removes the worst-case penalty of a mistimed defense)
- **Partial Veil**: "Redirect 30% of physical damage to MP" — lets non-Veil characters dip into the Mana Veil mechanic
- "Immune to Bleed" or "Burnout duration halved" (status resistance)
- "Reduce Reserve overflow by 20%" (softens the penalty for over-spending)

### Accessories

Bridge systems and create cross-mechanic synergies.

Example effects:
- "Successful counters refund 10 Active MP" (connects Kael's counter play to magical sustain)
- "S-rank casts grant Inspired to self" (connects casting excellence to action economy)
- "Active HP overflow to Reserve is doubled" (amplifies the defensive-play-heals-you mechanic)
- "Rest recovers 5% more Reserve" (stronger explicit Rest)
- "Groove Meter fills 20% faster" (rewards frequent casting)
- "Full Groove Meter also widens defense windows for 2 turns" (bridges magic → defense)

## What Equipment Explicitly Does NOT Do

- No "+X Attack" / "+X Defense" flat stats
- No equipment that trivializes execution (no "all hits count as Perfect")
- No equipment that replaces character identity (no full Mana Veil via equipment — Partial Veil caps at 30–40% redirection)
- No equipment that is universally optimal — every piece should be a trade-off or a situational pick

## Equipment Acquisition

Equipment is found through the same exploration-driven philosophy as Chain Strikes and Sheet Music:
- **Shops**: basic equipment with straightforward modifiers. Available in towns.
- **Crafting**: combine found materials into specific pieces. Materials are exploration rewards.
- **Treasure**: unique equipment in dungeons, hidden areas, and quest rewards. The most interesting tactical modifiers come from exploration.
- **Boss drops**: signature equipment from defeated bosses with thematically appropriate effects.

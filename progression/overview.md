# Progression — Overview

## Core Philosophy

Progression is **horizontal** (more options) not **vertical** (bigger numbers). A level 1 character with a skilled player should be able to beat content far above their level, because stats don't scale damage and execution quality is the true multiplier. Leveling makes fights *easier to manage* (more HP cushion, more recovery breathing room, more Chain Strike and spell options), not categorically possible.

The player gets stronger by **finding new tools** and **mastering old ones**, not by watching numbers go up.

## Stat Scaling — The Flat Curve

Stats grow by **5–10% total over the entire game**. A character with 85 Vitality at level 1 might have 92 Vitality at the level cap. This is intentional: the stat spread is a character's *identity*, not something that normalizes over time.

- Each level grants **1 stat point**, distributed automatically per character along their archetype curve (e.g., Ren gains Vitality or Stamina, never Magic; Sera gains Magic or Spirit).
- Level cap is **30–40**, meaning stats move by ~30–40 points total spread across 5 stats — roughly 6–8 points per stat on an 0–100 scale.
- **No stat respec.** Characters are who they are. Equipment handles tactical flexibility.

Why this works with the foundational rule: since move damage is fixed and execution quality is the real scaler, a +7 Vitality over the whole game just means Ren can absorb about 7 more points of overflow before dying. It is a comfort margin, not a power spike.

## Four Sources of Progression

| Source | What it gives | Where it comes from | Details |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Chain Strike Acquisition](chain-strike-acquisition.md) | New physical combo options | World items, quests, boss drops | Found, not leveled into |
| [Sheet Music](sheet-music-acquisition.md) | New spells | Exploration, NPCs, puzzles | Replaces spell shops entirely |
| [Equipment](equipment-and-loadout.md) | Tactical modifiers | Crafting, shops, treasure | Options, not power |
| [Mastery](mastery-system.md) | Deeper execution options | Use-based practice | Skill ceiling rises, power floor stays |

## What Progression Is NOT

- **Not power curves.** A character at level 30 with the same Chain Strikes as level 1 deals the same damage — they just have slightly more HP cushion.
- **Not mandatory.** A player who ignores side content and rushes the story has fewer options but is not locked out of progress. The base kit (starter Chain Strike + basic spells) is sufficient for the critical path with good execution.
- **Not grinding.** No encounter in the game should require the player to fight the same enemies repeatedly to gain levels. Every stat point, Chain Strike, spell, and piece of equipment is available through exploration, quests, and story progression.
